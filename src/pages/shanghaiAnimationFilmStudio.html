Shanghai Animation Film Studio

Introduction

Established in 1957, Shanghai Animation Film Studio (Shanghai Animation) stands as a beacon of artistic expression and cultural heritage, etching its name among the oldest and most revered animation studios in China. Nestled in the heart of Shanghai's Changning District, at 618 Wanhong Road, the studio has served as a creative powerhouse, breathing life into captivating stories and nurturing the imaginations of generations.

Classic Animated Works

Shanghai Animation has crafted a plethora of timeless animated classics renowned for their distinctive artistic flair and exceptional production quality. Notable works include "Havoc in Heaven," "The Little Tadpole Looking for Mom," "The Black Cat Sheriff," and "The Calabash Brothers," among others, cherished by audiences far and wide.

Historical Background

The precursor to Shanghai Animation Film Studio was the Cartoon Stock of Northeast Film Studio, established in 1949. Officially founded as Shanghai Animation Film Studio in 1957, its creative ensemble comprises seasoned artists alongside a cohort of young, burgeoning talent. This amalgamation has fostered a legacy of creativity, research, and the cultivation of a unique artistic identity.

Diverse Productions

Shanghai Animation Film Studio showcases versatility through its diverse repertoire, spanning feature films, educational content, opera adaptations, musical animations, and more. Its productions have not only garnered domestic acclaim but have also received numerous accolades on the international stage.

Cultural Significance

Regarded as a treasure trove of Chinese animation art, Shanghai Animation's creations are esteemed as cultural gems of the Chinese nation, embodying the essence of China's rich artistic heritages of the Chinese nation.
