Introduction of the movie

    The Great Pandemonium is China's first color animated feature film, one of the pinnacle works of Chinese animation, and one of the classic masterpieces of the Chinese school of animation. Based on the first seven stories of the Chinese classic “Journey to the West”, the film uses animation to portray the classic Chinese mythological hero, the Monkey King.

    The artistic style of the film is rich in Chinese national characteristics and traditional cultural connotations. On the basis of the decorative style, the film also integrates the animation's unique animation and imaginative creative techniques, touching people's hearts with art and impressing the world with cultural connotations, which is an important milestone in the history of China's animation development.

The Story of Princess Iron Fan

Princess Iron Fan, also known as Princess Rakshasa, is a character in the Chinese novel Journey to the West. She is the wife of the Bull Demon King and the mother of Red Boy. She possesses a magic banana fan that can create powerful winds and flames.

The story of Princess Iron Fan can be summarized as follows:

  • Sun Wukong Borrows the Banana Fan: Sun Wukong needs to borrow Princess Iron Fan's banana fan to extinguish the flames of Fire Mountain. He visits Princess Iron Fan three times, but she refuses to lend him the fan each time.
  • Sun Wukong Tricks Princess Iron Fan: Sun Wukong learns that Princess Iron Fan's son, Red Boy, enjoys eating humans. He disguises himself as a child and tricks Red Boy into trusting him. Using his cunning, Sun Wukong subdues Red Boy and uses their relationship to finally borrow the banana fan from Princess Iron Fan.
  • Sun Wukong Borrows the Banana Fan Again: After borrowing the banana fan, Sun Wukong discovers that Princess Iron Fan has given him a fake fan. He returns to her dwelling and engages in a fierce battle with the Bull Demon King. Ultimately, Sun Wukong defeats the Bull Demon King and successfully borrows the real banana fan.

The story of Princess Iron Fan showcases Sun Wukong's intelligence, bravery, and unwavering determination in the face of adversity. It also reflects the richness and diversity of characters and stories found in ancient Chinese mythology.

  • Princess Iron Fan is believed to be based on the Chinese wind deity from ancient mythology.

In the story of "Journey to the West," Sun Wukong, also known as the Monkey King, has a significant connection to the Flower Fruit Mountain.

  1. Birth of Sun Wukong: Sun Wukong was initially born as a stone monkey nurtured by a rock. He was born and grew up on the Flower Fruit Mountain, hence being referred to as the Monkey of Flower Fruit Mountain's Water Curtain Cave.
  2. Monkey King: Due to his intelligence, agility, and cleverness, Sun Wukong quickly became the leader of the monkey tribe on Flower Fruit Mountain, earning the title of Monkey King.
  3. Seeking Immortality: Sun Wukong yearned for eternal life and sought to obtain the power of immortality. He left Flower Fruit Mountain and embarked on a journey to find enlightenment and immortality. Along his journey, he encountered various gods and practitioners, learning numerous spells and martial arts.
  4. The Four Great Heavenly Kings: During Sun Wukong's training, he encountered the Four Great Heavenly Kings, who were celestial guards from the Heavenly Court. They were known as the Blue, Red, Yellow, and Green Four Great Heavenly Kings. After engaging in battles with them, Sun Wukong eventually became friends with them, gaining their appreciation and support.
  5. Conflict with Demon Kings: Sun Wukong's growing reputation on Flower Fruit Mountain attracted the attention of the Heavenly Court. The Jade Emperor dispatched celestial soldiers and generals to capture Sun Wukong. However, Sun Wukong, with his martial arts skills and magic, fought fiercely against them and successfully defended Flower Fruit Mountain.
  6. Sun Wukong as Protector of Tang Sanzang: Later, at the behest of the Jade Emperor, Sun Wukong was sent to the mortal realm to protect the Buddhist monk Tang Sanzang on his journey to retrieve sacred scriptures. Sun Wukong, along with Tang Sanzang, Zhu Bajie, and Sha Wujing, embarked on a perilous journey, facing various adventures and trials.
