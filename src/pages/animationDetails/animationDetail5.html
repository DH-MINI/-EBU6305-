Introduction of the movie

    The collection consists of eight separate stories rooted in traditional Chinese culture: 'The Summer of the Little Monster', 'Goose Goose Goose', 'Lin Lin', 'The Country Bus Takes Away the King's Child and the Immortals', 'Xiao Man', 'The Jade Rabbit', 'The Commissary', 'The Flying Bird and the Fish', which spreads out in front of the audience a collection of stories about the 'Monster' with a strong Chinese imagination and aesthetic appeal. The stories are a collection of 'monster' stories with a strong Chinese imagination and aesthetic appeal.

    The stories, ranging from ancient tales to science fiction imagery, from local fondness to beautiful love, from life's themes to human nature reflections, demonstrate Chinese imagination and carry Chinese national culture and philosophy. The film covers a wide range of art styles and production techniques: from traditional 2D, paper-cutting and even stop-motion animation, to modern techniques such as CG and 3D rendering, as well as innovative attempts to combine sketching with Chinese ink and wash, which is a multi-perspective interpretation of Chinese aesthetics by the creators.

Little Big Mouth's Summer": A Journey of Self-Discovery and Unexpected Friendship
The Journey Begins
  • Unfulfilled Dreams: Little Big Mouth dreams of a bigger role in the Moon Festival banquet.
  • Unexpected Opportunity: He encounters Wukong, the imprisoned Monkey King.
  • Unlikely Friendship: Little Big Mouth and Wukong form a bond.
Facing Challenges
  • Overcoming Obstacles: Little Big Mouth faces ridicule, doubts, and harsh realities.
  • Finding Strength in Friendship: Wukong's guidance helps him overcome fears and embrace potential.
  • Discovering His True Value: Little Big Mouth proves his worth and earns respect.
Themes and Significance
  • Self-Discovery: The film emphasizes believing in oneself and realizing potential.

Goose, Goose, Goose": A Journey Through Dreamlike Encounters

The Narrative Journey

  • A Simple Premise: A boy sent to buy goose meat encounters strange events.
  • Surreal Encounters: Talking goose, goose-headed woman, and synchronized geese.
  • Open to Interpretation: Ambiguous ending leaves room for interpretation.

Themes and Symbolism

  • Power of Imagination: The film celebrates imagination and finding wonder in the everyday.
  • Nature of Reality: Explores the subjective nature of reality and blurred lines between dreams and waking life.
  • Coming-of-Age: The boy's journey can be interpreted as a coming-of-age story.

Birds and Fish": A Tale of Forbidden Love and Unfulfilled Dreams
The Forbidden Love
  • Star-Crossed Lovers: Kun, a bird, and Fei, a fish, fall in love despite their different worlds.
  • Obstacles and Challenges: Societal norms and natural limitations pose significant obstacles.
  • The Pursuit of Dreams: Kun and Fei dream of a life together, but their dreams seem impossible.
The Bittersweet Ending
  • The Ultimate Sacrifice: Kun and Fei transform into each other's species, sacrificing their identities.
  • Unfulfilled Dreams: Their transformation fails to achieve their dreams, leaving them with a sense of loss.
  • A Lesson in Acceptance: The ending highlights the importance of acceptance and the challenges of overcoming limitations.
