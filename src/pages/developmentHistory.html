Development History

China's first ink cartoon

In 1960, he created a new process of ink and wash animation, combining elegant Chinese ink and wash painting with animated films to form the most Chinese artistic style. Under the guidance of Tewei and Qian Jiajun, the animators represented by Tang Cheng, Wu Qiang, Yan Dingxian, Xu Jingda, and the photographers represented by Duan Xiaoxuan, Wang Shirong, and You Yong put traditional Chinese ink paintings on the screen and filmed the first Chinese ink cartoon "Little Tadpoles Looking for their Mother", which is well known in the world.

The year 1960

China's first widescreen animated feature film

Made in 1979 to celebrate the 30th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China, Nezha is China's first wide-screen animated feature film and once again radiates the glory of the national style with its majestic representation. Based on parts of the classic novel Romance of the Gods, Nezha tells the story of a feud between Nezha, the son of Li Jing in Chentang Pass, and the Dragon Palace in the East China Sea. With rich imagination, the film revolves around the four important links of Nezha's "birth, death, regeneration and disturbance", which progress layer by layer and are gripping. The film has a beautiful picture and a touching plot, and has received a lot of praise when it was released at home and abroad.

The year 1979

Black Cat Detective

Black Cat Sheriff is a two-dimensional animation series co-directed by Dai Tielang, Fan Madi and Xiong Nanqing and produced by Shanghai Fine Arts Film Studio. The witty and brave black cat sheriff and his police partners truded hamsters, captured ferocious monkey hawks in the air, caught elephants, hippos, and wild boars stealing red soil, fought wits and courage with one ear, detected one case after another, and defended the safety of the forest.
