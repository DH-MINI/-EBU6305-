Development History

China's first ink cartoon

China's art films began in the early 1920s, when the “Wan Brothers” shot China's first batch of animated films in Shanghai. 1949 saw the establishment of an organization specializing in the production of art films, the Art Film Group, at Changchun's Northeastern Film Studio, with cartoonist Te Wei and painter Jin Xi as its main leaders. cartoonist Te Wei and painter Jin Xi were the main leaders.

The year 1960

China's first ink cartoon

In 1960, he created a new process of ink and wash animation, combining elegant Chinese ink and wash painting with animated films to form the most Chinese artistic style. Under the guidance of Tewei and Qian Jiajun, the animators represented by Tang Cheng, Wu Qiang, Yan Dingxian, Xu Jingda, and the photographers represented by Duan Xiaoxuan, Wang Shirong, and You Yong put traditional Chinese ink paintings on the screen and filmed the first Chinese ink cartoon "Little Tadpoles Looking for their Mother", which is well known in the world.

The year 1960

Uproar in Heaven

Uproar in Heaven is a two-dimensional animated movie directed by Wan Lai Ming and produced by Shanghai Fine Arts Film Studio. It is divided into two parts, the upper part was completed in 1961, and the lower part was completed in 1964, which is a monument in the history of Chinese animation. The film takes the experience of Sun Wukong in Journey to the West from his birth to the section of making a fuss in the Heavenly Palace as the main content. Through the story of Sun Wukong's making a fuss in the Dragon Palace and opposing the Heavenly Court, the legendary experience of the main character, Sun Wukong, is more centrally and prominently expressed. The whole film has strong color, strange shape, majestic and magnificent scenes, distinctive image features, and a unique plot, with unique artistic color, which can be called a monumental work of Chinese animation, and as a classic of Chinese animation affecting several generations, it is a monument in the history of Chinese animation.

The year 1961、1964

China's first widescreen animated feature film

Made in 1979 to celebrate the 30th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China, Nezha is China's first wide-screen animated feature film and once again radiates the glory of the national style with its majestic representation. Based on parts of the classic novel Romance of the Gods, Nezha tells the story of a feud between Nezha, the son of Li Jing in Chentang Pass, and the Dragon Palace in the East China Sea. With rich imagination, the film revolves around the four important links of Nezha's "birth, death, regeneration and disturbance", which progress layer by layer and are gripping. The film has a beautiful picture and a touching plot, and has received a lot of praise when it was released at home and abroad.

The year 1979

The Story of Avanti

The Story of Avanti is a puppet animation series produced by Shanghai Fine Arts Film Studio starting in 1979, directed by Qu Jianfang. It first produced one episode of the short animated movie “Avanti” [2], which was about 30 minutes long, and then produced a 13-episode series of animated movies “The Story of Avanti” (each episode has a sub-title) [3], with a total of 14 episodes. With the legendary character Avanti (i.e. Hogarth Nasruddin) as the main character, the entire animated movie is divided into a dozen short stories. The funny and humorous image of Afanti riding a donkey upside down and his jokes that make people can't help but laugh are not only a household name among the Uyghur people in China, but also very familiar to people of all ethnic groups in China, and have been widely circulated around the world.

The year 1979

Black Cat Detective

Black Cat Sheriff is a two-dimensional animation series co-directed by Dai Tielang, Fan Madi and Xiong Nanqing and produced by Shanghai Fine Arts Film Studio. The witty and brave black cat sheriff and his police partners truded hamsters, captured ferocious monkey hawks in the air, caught elephants, hippos, and wild boars stealing red soil, fought wits and courage with one ear, detected one case after another, and defended the safety of the forest.

The year 1984

Calabash Brothers

Calabash Brothers is a 13-episode series of paper-cut animated cartoons originally produced by Shanghai Fine Arts Film Studio in 1985-1987, one of the representative works of the second boom period of Chinese animation, which has become a classic of Chinese animation. It tells the story of seven magical gourds, seven brothers with superb skills, who go forward and backward to save their relatives, and start a circle with the goblins. The Hulu Brothers is one of the original classic animations in China, which has been a favorite of the audience, especially children and teenagers, since its broadcast in 1986.

The year 1985

Shuck and Bertha.

Shuck and Bertha is an animated film produced by Shanghai Fine Arts Film Studio, directed by Yan Dingxian and written by Yao Zhongli and Nie Xinru. The film is based on the novel of Zheng Yuanjie, the king of fairy tales, and tells the story of Shuko and Beta, the rats who bear the reputation of “thieves” and fly airplanes and tanks to help others, defeat pirates, and finally defend the peace of mankind.

The year 1989

Lotus Lantern

Pauline Lamp is an animated movie directed by Chang Guangxi and voiced by Jiang Wen, Ning Jing, Chen Peisi, Zhu Shimao, Xu Fan, Yu Pengfei, Nie Xiaozhe, Yang Shuo, Ma Xiaoqing, Ma Antelope, Ding Jiali, Zhao Yiou . Based on the Chinese myth of the same name, the movie tells the story of Shenxiang, who learns from a master in order to rescue her mother, who has been crushed under Mount Hua, and ultimately defeats her uncle Erlangshen to save her mother by means of the Po Lin Lamp.

The year 1999

Big-eared Tutu

Big Ears Tutu is an animated television series directed by Speed, written by Tinkerbell, voiced by Doudou, Fan Chuzhong and Zhao Ming, and produced by Shanghai Fine Arts Film Studio. The series consists of five seasons of animated television and two animated movies, and focuses on the growth story of Hu Tutu and his friends. Hututu is a witty, naughty and smart three-year-old who is naturally happy, kind-hearted, nosy, energetic, very curious about things around him, he loves to think nonsense, and his best feature is that he is delicious, and he is so cute and smart that people can't help but laugh at him.
